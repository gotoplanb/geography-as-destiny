## The Reconstructed Sequence (Dave Stanton)

PIE became widespread as a lingua franca through a specific three-phase sequence operating across the Dnieper-Volga geographic substrate. The sequence runs:

**Phase 1 — Federation crystallization (~5000-4200 BCE).** The Dnieper-Volga federation stabilizes a large internal-cooperation zone between two river systems. The Dnieper provides the western contact with Old European farming populations. The Volga provides the eastern steppe depth. The federation between them develops the PIE lingua franca because the commercial logic — seasonal trade fairs, koryos raiding economies, bride-price marriage networks, federation-from-operational-range cooperation (card 195) — demands a common coordination medium across the operational range. Raid and trade west to the Danube. Raid and trade east to the Urals. Relatively stable mutual exchange characterizes the period.

**Phase 2 — Power explosion (~4200-3500 BCE).** The climate forcing function (card 188) tips the balance. The ~4200 BCE cold event reduces cattle viability while horse populations scale on the grass resource base. The mobile military advantage suddenly dramatically exceeds anything the farming communities to the west can match. The pressure differential crosses the threshold (card 194). The seasonal trade fair relationship with Old Europe tips from exchange to conflict. The fortifications go up. The hybrid pottery production stops.

**Phase 3 — Simultaneous expansion (~3500-2500 BCE).** The federation's accumulated energy releases in multiple directions simultaneously. West — the Yamnaya wave overrunning Old Europe, carrying PIE into the farming communities. East — the Afanasievo leapfrog to the Altai, eventually producing the Tocharian populations in the Tarim Basin. Southeast — the Indo-Iranian branch moving toward the Iranian plateau and the subcontinent. The lingua franca traveled with the federation's expansion. Not as deliberate policy — as the coordination medium the raiding-and-trading economy had already made universal within the federation zone, now spreading into the contact populations on both sides.

## The Critical Timing Claim

The three-phase sequence makes a specific Saussure-mode prediction about PIE's relationship to the Yamnaya expansion: **PIE crystallized during the federation phase, not during the expansion phase.** The expansion carried it outward. The lingua franca was already operating across the Dnieper-Volga federation zone before horses enabled outward projection at continental scale.

This timing claim is testable through the linguistic evidence. PIE vocabulary should preserve the federation-phase institutional terms — cattle raiding, bride price, seasonal trade fairs, youth warrior bands (koryos — card 195). Expansion-phase vocabulary — conquest terms, subjugation terms, sedentary-administration terms — should appear later in the daughter languages as they diverge from the common source, reflecting the conditions the expanding populations encountered rather than the conditions of the original federation.

If this prediction holds, it tells the framework something important about how lingua francas form: **they crystallize during stable productive-exchange-phase zones with sufficient internal cooperation, not during the expansion that subsequently carries them outward.** The Sogdian lingua franca crystallized during the productive Silk Road tidal-system phase, then traveled with Sogdian commerce. The Comanche lingua franca crystallized during the productive Plains federation phase, then spread through raiding and trading networks. The pattern is consistent: lingua francas are federation-phase outputs, not expansion-phase outputs.

The Yamnaya expansion was the *carrier mechanism* for PIE, not the *creation mechanism*. The federation that preceded the expansion did the creation work.
