# East Turkestan / Xinjiang — The Gap in Coverage and Source Leads

**Source:** Beta seminar discussion, 2026-04-14
**Type:** connection
**Relevant to:** Chapter 4 (Silk Road — eastern delta), global coverage discipline (ref 019)

## Content

East Turkestan / Xinjiang tends to get covered as a chapter in broader China histories or Silk Road books — never as the center. There's no Great Courses equivalent focused on this region specifically. That gap is itself an argument for the book: the geographic framework applied to the Tarim Basin would be genuinely original territory.

**Source leads (parked for future semesters):**
- **Valerie Hansen, *The Silk Road: A New History*** — archaeologically grounded, focused on Tarim Basin oasis cities (Dunhuang, Turfan, Khotan). Works from actual documents and artifacts rather than macro narrative. More East Turkestan-focused than most Silk Road books.
- **Justin Rudelson, *Oasis Identities*** — anthropological work on Uyghur culture specifically. Older but serious.
- **Darren Byler** — contemporary work on the surveillance state in Xinjiang. Rigorous. (Modern friction collapse applied to population control — potential Chapter 8 material.)

**Editorial note from Beta:** Always define geographic scope early and explicitly. Tasar assumed the Soviet/Russian framing was implicit; stating it upfront would have oriented listeners. Apply this lesson to the book — each chapter should define its geographic scope in the first paragraph.

## Why this matters

The eastern half of the Silk Road corridor is currently underrepresented in our evidence base. The Gansu Corridor (15 miles wide, Great Wall along its edge) is one of the most dramatic chokepoints on earth and it's in the table of contents but has no reference cards yet. Hansen could be a semester 2 or 3 source if a Silk Road semester emerges.

The Uyghur surveillance state is also a potential modern case for Chapter 8 — technology as friction collapse applied to population control rather than territorial conquest. Same dynamic, different application. But that's future-semester material.
