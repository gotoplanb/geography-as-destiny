## Content

**The prediction:** The Comanche in 1870 were roughly where the Xiongnu were in 300 BCE — powerful, mobile, extractive, beginning institutional complexity but still in the early raiding-dominant phase. Given centuries rather than decades, the developmental trajectory suggests: more sophisticated border management → tributary relationships → formalized commercial networks → the same progression that characterized Xiongnu → Han interaction.

The railroad terminated this trajectory before it could mature. Not because the trajectory was impossible but because the second friction collapse arrived before the first adaptation completed.

**Independent scholarly confirmation:** A reviewer of Hämäläinen (Haquelebac blog) independently reached the same conclusion:

> "A comparison can also be made with the Bulgar and Khazar trade states... These states were founded by nomad raiders akin to the Huns, but they eventually became nodes in a relatively peaceful trade network... These states were much larger and longer-lived than Comancheria, but they suggest a development trajectory which might have been possible under different historical circumstances."

The framework generated this prediction from first principles. An independent reviewer reached it from Hämäläinen's own material. Neither knew about the other's analysis.

**The "docking" concept (from academic steppe scholarship):** "For permanent success... it was insufficient to only rely on spreading terror with fire and sword. In order to increase their success the steppe nomads depended on building durable, ideally even political relations to the empires of the agricultural civilizations." The Comanche Arkansas River trade center was an early docking node. The railroad made the docking irrelevant.

**The grass-as-granary insight:** The North African adapted Spanish horse varieties converted arid Plains grass directly into military and transportation capacity. No grain storage, no conversion processing, no infrastructure vulnerability. The horse's metabolism IS the granary. Same energy economy as the Eurasian steppe — enormous self-replenishing energy base in grass converted to mobility via horse metabolism.

## Why this is Appendix B, not Chapter 6

The counterfactual ("given centuries, the Comanche would have developed Xiongnu-level institutional complexity") is framework-derived and independently confirmed but not provable. Chapter 6 can note the truncated trajectory and the parallel to Eurasian steppe development. The full counterfactual argument — what would have happened without the railroad — belongs in the appendix as a framework prediction about a path not taken.
