## The Observation

**The cliché:** "People become more conservative as they age." Sender-side encoding carrying political freight. Explains almost nothing mechanistically.

**The reframe:** People revert to the cultural substrate formed during their reminiscence bump — the period of heightened memory encoding in teens and twenties when identity and worldview are most plastic. The specific content of the reversion is not universally "conservative" — it is whatever was culturally dominant during each generation's formative window.

**The implications:**
- Boomers reverting to WWII good-versus-evil nation-state framing — because that narrative was the dominant cultural substrate during their formative years
- Millennials reverting in 40 years to 1990s post-Cold War globalist optimism or post-9/11 security anxiety — depending on which window dominated their encoding
- Each subsequent generation reverting to whatever the 2010s-2020s embedded — which the 2060s will find equally baffling

The "conservative" label gets applied because the dominant cultural substrate during most living generations' formative years happened to be more nationally and traditionally oriented than the subsequent liberal internationalist consensus. That is historically contingent, not structurally necessary. The reversion mechanism is universal; the specific content is generationally variable.

**Connection to card 139:** The reminiscence bump reversion is the encoding persisting past the point where the physical and social reality has rotated away from it. The aging person is not becoming irrational — they are applying a cultural encoding that was accurate and functional during the period when it was formed. The world has rotated; the encoding has not. Same orthogonal relationship, longer timescale.

## The Falsifiable Prediction

Track political attitude changes across generations controlling for age. Look for whether the specific content of late-life attitude reversion correlates with the dominant cultural narrative during the reminiscence bump rather than with a universal "conservative" direction. If the mechanism is reminiscence bump reversion rather than age-driven conservatism, each generation's reversion should look different from the previous one's — same direction (toward formative-era encoding), different destination.
