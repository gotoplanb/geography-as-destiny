# Appendix B Concept: Where the Framework Doesn't Apply — Faster Horses

**Source:** Alpha discussion, 2026-04-18
**Type:** connection
**Relevant to:** Book structure, methodology, Charlie's issue #4 (friction collapse boundaries)

## Content

A second appendix collecting cases that look like they fit the framework but fail the criteria on examination. These are "faster horses" — improvements that don't invert terrain function, decouple a constraint layer, or remove a geographic dependency.

**First candidate:** Jet stream routing in transatlantic aviation. Modern flight planning optimizes around the jet stream the way Silk Road merchants optimized around seasonal conditions. But this is operational optimization, not friction collapse. It doesn't change who the terrain serves. It doesn't decouple a layer. It's a faster horse. The three-criteria taxonomy correctly excludes it.

**Purpose:** 
1. Shows the framework has real boundaries — it's not a universal lens you can force onto everything
2. Demonstrates the falsifiability criteria working in practice (they exclude cases, not just include them)
3. Gives readers and critics examples of what the framework explicitly does NOT claim
4. Invites contributions — readers will come with "but what about X?" cases, and many of those will be faster horses rather than friction collapses

**Paired with Appendix A (Speculative Connections, ref 041):**
- Appendix A: "We think this might be true but can't prove it yet" (speculative predictions)
- Appendix B: "We tested this and the framework correctly excluded it" (boundary cases)

Together they make the falsifiability argument concrete rather than abstract.
