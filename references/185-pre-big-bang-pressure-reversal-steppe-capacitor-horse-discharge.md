## The Causal Sequence (Dave Stanton)

Card 164 named horse domestication as the framework's Big Bang — the first geographic orthogonal rotation, the temporal anchor where the framework's specialized dynamics begin operating. Card 165 distinguished the pre-Big Bang period (climate-driven slow dynamics) from the post-Big Bang period (mobility-driven fast dynamics). Both cards treated the Big Bang as the originating event for the framework's apparatus.

Dave's extension specifies what the pre-Big Bang universe actually did to *cause* the Big Bang event. The pre-Big Bang period is not just a slower-mechanism prelude. It is the system that compressed the energy the Big Bang would eventually discharge.

The causal sequence runs in three phases:

**Phase 1 — Eastward farming pressure (~7000-4500 BCE).** Carpathian piedmont and Anatolian farming communities accumulate surplus production capacity. Population grows. Pressure builds. The farming wave moves eastward, pushing into the Pontic-Caspian zone from the west and south. This is the Anatolian farmer expansion card 169 documents as the second of three European population waves. The pressure gradient runs eastward from the farming heartland toward the steppe.

**Phase 2 — The Big Bang event (~4500-4000 BCE).** Somewhere in the transitional zone between the farming frontier and the open steppe — the friction zone where piedmont-style containment terrain met the steppe's grass resource base — a population acquired and scaled horse domestication. The horse hit the open steppe. Horse populations exploded because the grass resource base was effectively unlimited at the scales that mattered. The mobile pastoralist toolkit suddenly became extraordinarily powerful.

**Phase 3 — Pressure gradient reversal (~4000-2500 BCE).** The steppe culture with horse mobility now has military and economic advantages over the farming communities to the west and south that had previously been the pressure source. The gradient flips. What had been the receiving end of farming expansion becomes the origin point of a new and faster pressure wave moving in the opposite direction. The Yamnaya expansion westward back to the Danube is the reversed pressure wave hitting the farming communities that had been its original source. Simultaneously, the wave continues eastward from the Urals because steppe terrain extends that direction with no comparable resistance.

## The Capacitor-Discharge Metaphor

The thermodynamic structure is elegantly precise. The farming expansion compressed energy into the steppe frontier zone. The horse domestication converted that compressed energy into a dramatically amplified return wave that traveled faster and further than the original.

**The steppe was the capacitor. The horse was the discharge mechanism.**

A capacitor stores electrical potential. A discharge mechanism converts the stored potential into rapid energy transfer when activated. The pre-Big Bang farming expansion stored potential in the steppe frontier zone in the form of accumulated population, commercial contact, and technological exchange. The horse domestication activated the discharge — converting the stored potential into an outward energy transfer that flowed back through the gradient that had originally compressed it, amplified by the mobility multiplier the horse provided.

This is the framework's most thermodynamically precise statement to date of why the Big Bang produced the specific outward directionality the Yamnaya expansion exhibits. The wave didn't go outward in arbitrary directions. It went outward along the same gradient the pre-Big Bang pressure had compressed, but reversed and amplified.

## Why the Two-Direction Expansion

The capacitor-discharge metaphor explains why the Yamnaya expansion ran in the specific two directions Anthony documents:

- **Westward back to the Danube and into Europe.** The pre-Big Bang farming pressure had originated west and south of the steppe (Carpathian piedmont, Anatolian uplands). The accumulated pressure at the steppe frontier was densest along the east-facing axes of the source populations. When the gradient reversed, the discharge flowed back along the same axes — but now hitting receiving populations that had previously been pressure sources. This is the Corded Ware and Bell Beaker overlay across central and western Europe.

- **Eastward into the Altai and the Tarim Basin.** Open steppe terrain extends eastward from the Pontic-Caspian zone with comparable Goldilocks-zone conditions for horse-enabled pastoralism. The discharge in this direction encountered no comparable pressure source — just compatible terrain that supported leapfrog expansion (card 184). The Afanasievo cultural complex is the eastward leapfrog outpost, and the Tocharian populations are its eventual descendants in the Tarim Basin (card 172).

The third direction Anthony documents — southeastward into the Iranian plateau and the Indian subcontinent — is the gradient adjustment where the pastoralist toolkit transitioned to mixed-economy terrain. This is the Indo-Iranian branch of the PIE language family.

In each case, the discharge direction follows the path of least resistance from the high-pressure source toward terrain that the cultural toolkit could exploit. The framework's thermodynamic gradient following (card 179) operates precisely.

## What This Adds to the Big Bang Analogy

The Big Bang in cosmology is not a beginning ex nihilo. It is a transition in the universe's state — a moment when the conditions that had been compressing potential energy released that energy into the universe we now observe. Pre-Big Bang cosmology asks what the universe was like in the period that produced the Big Bang event. The questions are genuinely difficult; the energy compression mechanism is partially recoverable through indirect evidence (the cosmic microwave background, the distribution of structure, the residual gravitational wave evidence).

Card 185 applies the same structural reading to the framework's Big Bang. Horse domestication is not a beginning from nowhere. It is the discharge moment for energy compressed in a pre-Big Bang phase the framework can read through Anthony's apparatus and the framework's accumulated case-library reasoning. The cosmic-microwave-background equivalents are the Anatolian farmer genetic substrate detectable in modern European populations, the archaeological distribution of pre-Yamnaya material culture across the Carpathian piedmont, the linguistic loanwords from pre-PIE substrates preserved in PIE itself.

**The pre-Big Bang universe created the Big Bang.** The framework now has a discrete causal story for why the Big Bang event occurred where it did, when it did, and produced the specific outward directionality it produced.

## What This Implies for the Framework

Three additions to the framework's apparatus:

1. **The pre-Big Bang phase is now a discrete causal element in the framework's apparatus, not just a slower-mechanism prelude.** The pressure that the horse domestication discharged was accumulated by the pre-Big Bang farming expansion. Future framework readings of the Big Bang event should treat the pre-event period as causally active, not just as initial conditions.

2. **The capacitor-discharge metaphor adds thermodynamic precision to the framework's vocabulary.** Where the framework previously used general gradient-following language, card 185 specifies a particular sub-mechanism — pressure accumulation followed by discharge, with the discharge amplified by mobility technology. This sub-mechanism may apply to other framework cases: the Atlantic friction collapse may have similar pre-collapse capacitor properties; the railroad friction collapse on the Plains may have been a discharge of pressure that the Spanish-Mexican period had accumulated.

3. **The two-direction expansion is now structurally explained.** Why the Yamnaya expansion ran westward back to the Danube and eastward into the Altai is not arbitrary — it follows the gradient axes the pre-Big Bang pressure had compressed, reversed and amplified by horse mobility. The third direction (southeastward into Iran) is the gradient adjustment to terrain the cultural toolkit could exploit. Same mechanism, three specific manifestations.
