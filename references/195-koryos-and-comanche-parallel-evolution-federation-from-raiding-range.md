## The Observation (Dave Stanton)

Anthony documents reconstructed Proto-Indo-European vocabulary for cattle raiding, bride price, and youth warrior bands (the *koryos*). These are not cultural curiosities. They are the institutional vocabulary of a specific social technology — one that the Comanche confederation operated four thousand years later on a different continent with no plausible connection to the PIE tradition.

The mechanism Dave identified runs:

1. **Young men need cattle for bride price.** Status acquisition through marriageability requires accumulated livestock. Inherited status varies across young men; the marginal young man with no inherited herd faces a structural problem.
2. **Cattle acquisition requires raiding.** In the surplus-less mobile pastoralist economy, you cannot accumulate cattle fast enough through breeding alone. Raiding other groups is the available path to status acquisition for marginal young men.
3. **Raiding range expands with horse mobility.** Pre-horse raiding was foot-speed limited; range was small. Horse domestication multiplies operational range by a factor of five to ten (card 165). Raiding can suddenly project far.
4. **Extended raiding range creates internal-cooperation economics.** Raiding your immediate neighbors becomes economically irrational because you need their cooperation for defense against more distant raiders. The zone within your operational raiding radius becomes your alliance network. The raiding pressure goes outward, toward the zones beyond.
5. **The federation self-organizes from the bottom up.** No top-down ideological unification required. No great leader deciding to unite the tribes. The federation emerges from the practical logic of the raiding economy itself.

This is the framework's most analytically important institutional mechanism — and the PIE vocabulary preserves it from approximately 4000-3000 BCE while the Comanche demonstrated it operating in real time at approximately 1700-1875 CE.

## The Comanche Confirmation

The Comanche band confederation operated this mechanism exactly as the framework now predicts the koryos mechanism should operate. Documented evidence:

- **The Arkansas valley commercial hub** (cards 122, Chapter 6): bands within a certain operational radius converged on a shared trade center because cooperation extracted more value than internal raiding.
- **Outward-directed raiding** toward Apache settlements (card 114), Mexican haciendas (cards 121, 132, 137), and farther-distant targets — exactly what the framework predicts when internal raiding becomes economically irrational.
- **Bride price economy** documented through the Comanche captive trade and horse-acquisition patterns (cards 110, 138): young men needing horses and captives for status acquisition, raiding distant populations to obtain them.
- **Distributed authority** rather than centralized command (card 133): the federation operated as a confederation of bands rather than as a unified state, exactly the institutional form the framework predicts the federation-from-raiding-range mechanism produces.
- **The youth warrior bands** — the structurally equivalent institution to the PIE koryos — organized into raiding parties projecting outward while the internal zone stabilized into federation.

Same mechanism. Different continent. Different millennium. No plausible historical connection between the PIE-era steppe and the Comanche southern Plains.

## Parallel Evolution as Strongest Confirmation Type

The framework's apparatus for reading cross-case structural recurrence operates through several confirmation types:

- **Diffusion confirmation:** a mechanism documented in one case transmits to another through documented cultural contact (papermaking, bronze metallurgy — cards 168, 181). Convincing for the specific cases but requires demonstrable transmission pathway.
- **Convergent terminological confirmation:** independent scholars arrive at the same concept through different evidence (Hämäläinen's conjunction logic, Haquelebac's truncated trajectory, Anthony's robust frontiers — cards 158, 173, 174). Strong evidence but limited to terminological-level claims.
- **Multi-channel evidence convergence:** linguistics + archaeology + genetics converge on the same reconstruction (Kurgan hypothesis confirmation — card 189). Strong but limited to specific documented cases.
- **Parallel evolution confirmation:** identical institutional forms emerging independently from identical geographic-economic conditions in cases with no plausible historical connection. The framework's strongest confirmation type, because it eliminates diffusion, cultural transmission, and historical inheritance as alternative explanations.

The koryos/Comanche-federation case is the framework's clearest parallel-evolution confirmation. PIE-era steppe pastoralists developed cattle raiding, bride price institutions, and youth warrior bands. Comanche southern Plains pastoralists developed structurally identical institutions four thousand years later. No transmission pathway connects them. The terrain, the economic substrate (horse-enabled mobile pastoralism), and the demographic pressure (young men requiring status acquisition through raiding) were identical in both cases. The institutional response was identical in both cases.

**The steppe produces the koryos. Every time. Everywhere steppe conditions exist.**

## The General Mechanism Stated

The framework now has a discrete institutional-mechanism prediction the dissertation can deploy:

**Wherever (1) mobile pastoralism, (2) horse-enabled operational range, (3) surplus-less economy requiring raid-based status acquisition for marginal young men, and (4) demographic pressure exceeding local cooperation capacity all hold simultaneously, the framework predicts the emergence of cattle-raiding youth warrior bands operating outward against distant targets while internal-cooperation federation stabilizes within operational radius.**

This is testable across multiple cases:

- **PIE-era steppe (~4000-3000 BCE):** linguistic evidence of koryos institution (this card's primary case)
- **Scythian confederation (~700-200 BCE):** documented Greek and Persian accounts of similar institutions
- **Xiongnu confederation (~200 BCE-200 CE):** Chinese imperial accounts of similar institutions
- **Mongol confederation (~1200 CE):** documented evidence of nökör warrior bands operating exactly this mechanism
- **Comanche confederation (~1700-1875 CE):** documented evidence (this card's confirming case)
- **Plains Cherokee, Apache, Kiowa parallels:** similar institutional patterns documented in 19th century ethnography

Six independent cases of the same institutional mechanism across roughly six thousand years. The framework predicts the pattern should appear wherever the four structural conditions hold. Where the conditions hold and the institution does not appear, the framework needs refinement. Where the institution appears and the conditions do not hold, the framework needs explanation.

## Why This Confirms the Yamnaya-Prototype Claim

Card 171 named the Yamnaya horizon as the prototype every subsequent steppe confederation iterates on for five thousand years. Card 195 specifies the *mechanism* by which the iteration operates: the koryos institutional pattern, recurring whenever the four structural conditions are met, regardless of cultural transmission or historical connection.

The Yamnaya did not invent the federation-from-raiding-range mechanism through cultural genius. The mechanism is what the terrain-and-economic substrate selects for whenever horse-enabled mobile pastoralism encounters demographic pressure with marginal young men needing status acquisition through raid-based wealth accumulation. The Yamnaya were the first steppe population for which the conditions were met at scale. Every subsequent steppe confederation operated under similar conditions and produced the same institutional response.

Card 138's universal-status-acquisition mechanism is the demographic-pressure component of this larger institutional pattern. The young men needing status through raid-based wealth acquisition is what produces the koryos institution wherever the terrain supports it.
