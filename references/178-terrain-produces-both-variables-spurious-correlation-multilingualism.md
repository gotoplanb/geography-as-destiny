# Terrain Produces Both Variables — Spurious Correlation Through Geographic Common Cause

**Source:** Beta session synthesis (Dave Stanton observation), 2026-05-11. Anthony reading arc — methodological catch on an ethnolinguistic claim.
**Type:** methodology / causal misattribution pattern
**Relevant to:** Chapter 1 (framework / methodology), foreword (methodology), ref 053 (tributary system as sender-receiver encoding), ref 095 (phase-dependent encoding), ref 148 (hypocrisy as encoding mismatch), ref 153 (encoding slips in ethnohistory), ref 162 (theoretical/experimental physics methodology), ref 173 (persistent frontiers), ref 174 (robust frontiers / Anthony citation), ref 175 (sedentary nationalism projection)

## The Triggering Claim

Anthony cites an ethnolinguist who posits that **security of income affects number of languages spoken** — lower security correlating with more languages. The causal mechanism offered is psychological: insecurity creates a need for social connection, which motivates language learning, which produces multilingualism.

Dave's methodological catch: this is likely a spurious correlation. Security and multilingualism are probably both downstream effects of the same underlying geographic condition — operating at frontier zones — rather than causally linked to each other through the psychological intermediary.

## The Framework's Causal Alternative

The framework's reading offers a more parsimonious mechanism that explains both variables without requiring the psychological intermediary:

**Direct commercial incentive at the frontier.** Friction zones — robust frontiers, trading boundaries, material culture interfaces — reward multilingualism directly. The merchant who speaks three languages closes more deals. The diplomat who can navigate multiple cultural frameworks survives border politics better. The person who can communicate across the frontier extracts more value from being at the frontier. Multilingualism is an economic asset at the boundary, regardless of any psychological state.

**Self-selection at the frontier.** Risk-tolerant, curious, adaptable individuals are more likely to position themselves at frontier zones — because that is where opportunities concentrate for populations with those traits. The same personality profile that selects into frontier residence is more likely to invest in language acquisition as a tool for exploiting frontier opportunities. The selection effect compounds the direct incentive.

**Frontier insecurity as separate downstream effect.** Frontier zones are insecure because robust frontiers are contested by definition (card 174). The insecurity and the multilingualism are both products of the same underlying geographic condition. Neither causes the other.

Two effects sharing a common cause being mistaken for a direct causal relationship between the effects. Classic spurious correlation.

## The Methodological Move

This is a specific pattern of causal misattribution worth naming. It appears whenever scholarly work observes correlations between two variables that share a common geographic or structural cause, and produces a psychological causal story linking the two variables directly rather than identifying the common cause.

The framework's preferred diagnostic move:

1. Identify the correlation being reported
2. Ask whether both variables could be downstream effects of a common geographic or structural condition
3. If yes, check whether the common-cause hypothesis fits the same data as the direct-causation hypothesis
4. If both hypotheses fit the data, prefer the common-cause hypothesis under Occam's razor — it requires fewer causal links and does not require the psychological intermediary

This is not a critique of the ethnolinguist's careful empirical work. The correlation is probably real. The methodological catch is about the causal story attached to the correlation, not about the correlation itself.

## Why Psychological Framing Is the Default

Three reasons sender-side psychological framing operates automatically even in careful scholarly work, structurally analogous to card 175's sedentary nationalism projection:

1. **Psychological causation is the default cognitive frame for human observers.** We are evolved to attribute behavior to mental states. Observing a population doing something, we instinctively ask what motivates them, what they want, what they fear. This frame works well for individual-scale analysis. It misleads at population-scale analysis where structural mechanisms produce patterns that look psychological from the outside.

2. **Sender-side reporting amplifies psychological framing.** When the population being studied is described in terms of what they say or report or articulate, the description emphasizes their stated psychological states. The structural conditions producing the psychological states get backgrounded. The reporter ends up with rich descriptions of psychology and thin descriptions of structure — and the causal story builds on what is rich.

3. **The publication and citation incentive rewards psychological stories.** Psychological causal stories are more vivid, more relatable, more publishable than structural-geographic stories. They produce more memorable findings. They are easier to teach. They survive the citation Wald-bomber selection (card 154) better than structural alternatives that require more analytical apparatus to convey.

The framework's apparatus catches the misattribution because the framework is structurally oriented from the start. It asks first what terrain selects for and what structural conditions reward. Only after structural mechanisms are identified does the framework ask whether psychological states need to be invoked as causal intermediaries.

## Two Additional Examples Worth Cataloging

The pattern is not specific to the multilingualism case. Once named, it can be detected in other scholarly findings:

- **The "wealth produces conservatism" correlation.** Standard psychological causal story: wealth produces psychological investment in stability, which produces conservative voting. Framework's structural alternative: terrain types that produce stable wealth accumulation (suburban property ownership, agricultural land tenure, established commercial sectors) also produce political alignment patterns that read as conservative. Both effects (wealth and conservative voting) share a common terrain-structural cause. The psychological intermediary may not be necessary.

- **The "education correlates with liberal politics" finding.** Standard psychological causal story: education exposes individuals to liberal ideas, which shifts their politics. Framework's structural alternative: terrain types that produce concentrated education (urban centers, university towns, knowledge-economy regions — card 176's urban bundle) also produce political alignment patterns that read as liberal. Both effects share a common terrain-structural cause. The psychological intermediary may not be necessary.

In each case, the standard psychological causal story may be partially correct but is likely insufficient. The structural mechanism explains the correlation more parsimoniously. The framework's preference is to identify the structural mechanism first, then check whether psychological intermediaries add explanatory power beyond what the structure already provides.

## What This Implies for the Framework

Three additions to the framework's apparatus:

1. **Spurious correlation through geographic common cause is now a named methodological catch.** The framework can apply this diagnostic to any scholarly finding that reports a correlation with a psychological causal story attached. Where a structural alternative fits the same data, the framework prefers it under Occam's razor.

2. **The structural-over-psychological preference is now articulated explicitly.** The framework reads structure first. Psychology gets invoked only where structure alone cannot account for the observed pattern. This is consistent with the framework's broader analytical approach (cards 014, 138, 175) but card 178 names the preference as a methodological move that can be applied to specific scholarly findings.

3. **The dissertation can cite this kind of methodological catch as evidence of the framework's analytical value.** The framework does not just describe history. It produces specific diagnostic moves on contemporary scholarly findings. Each successful catch is evidence that the framework operates as a useful filter for distinguishing structural causes from psychological narratives that may be mistaking shared cause for direct causation.

## The Closing Synthesis

**The terrain produces the insecurity. The terrain produces the multilingualism. The insecurity doesn't produce the multilingualism.**

That is the framework's reading of the security-multilingualism correlation in three sentences. The pattern generalizes. Wherever scholarly work reports a psychological causal story linking two variables that could plausibly share a structural common cause, the framework's diagnostic asks whether the structural explanation fits the same data without the psychological intermediary. Where it does, Occam's razor prefers the structural explanation.

This is not anti-psychological. Psychological mechanisms are real and operate at individual scale. The methodological catch is about distinguishing individual-scale psychological causation from population-scale structural causation that produces patterns looking psychological from the outside. The framework's preference is to identify structural causes first and invoke psychological intermediaries only where the structure alone is insufficient.

**Worth noting as a methodological example of how sender-side psychological framing can obscure geographic causal mechanisms even in careful scholarly work.** Card 178 names the pattern. The framework can now apply it consistently.
