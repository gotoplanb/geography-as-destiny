## The Prediction

The framework predicts that Ancestral Puebloan, Mexica/Aztec, and California basin cultures share a common origin population that diverged along different resource gradients on opposite sides of the Rocky Mountains:

- Southward migration down the east side of the Rockies during drought (~1276-1299 CE) → Mexica in central Mexico
- Separate northward migration along the west side (California valley corridor) during wet periods → California basin cultures
- The Rockies as the divergence mechanism splitting one population into two culturally distinct but genetically related trajectories

**Testable:** Ancient DNA from Pueblo, central Mexican, and California populations should show closer genetic relationships than conventional cultural archaeology would predict, if the shared-origin-plus-divergent-gradient model is correct. The resource gradient sequence (climate-driven, terrain-channeled) should be visible in the genetic data.

**Secondary prediction:** The legendary "city of gold" may encode copper-based wealth markers mistranslated through the Spanish encoding layer. Archaeological evidence at candidate Aztlán sites (e.g., Chaco Canyon) should show copper rather than gold if the encoding distortion hypothesis holds.
