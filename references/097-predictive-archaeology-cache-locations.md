# Predictive Archaeology — The Framework Tells You Where to Dig

**Source:** Hansen, *The Silk Road* (epilogue), via Beta seminar 2026-04-23
**Type:** connection
**Relevant to:** Appendix A (future research — strongest testable prediction), Chapter 4 (Silk Road), ref 090 (missionary training center)

## Content

Hansen notes that some scholars believe more Silk Road artifacts remain to be found. That's an unfalsifiable belief. The framework converts it to a falsifiable hypothesis by predicting specific locations and the logic behind each.

**The prediction chain:**
1. The missionary training center model predicts that every gravity-well node with Buddhist administrative infrastructure faced the same Islamic conquest threat as Dunhuang
2. Communities facing that threat would have sealed and hidden document caches (same preservation behavior as the Dunhuang cave sealing ~1000 CE)
3. The Tarim Basin's hyperarid conditions provide preservation environments equal to or better than Dunhuang's
4. Therefore: sealed Buddhist document caches from the 10th-11th century CE should exist at specific nodes the framework identifies

**Predicted cache sites:**
- **Niya** — partially excavated (Stein), almost certainly more material in adjacent locations
- **Miran** — southern route oasis with known Buddhist infrastructure, only partially excavated
- **Khotan/Hotan** — gravity well of the southern route, largest Buddhist center before Islamic conquest
- **Kucha** — northern route gravity well, major Buddhist center with known manuscript production
- **Kroran/Loulan** — abandoned oasis city, potentially sealed material from the abandonment period

**Falsifiable:** If excavations at these nodes find sealed Buddhist document caches from the 10th-11th century, the missionary training center model gets extraordinarily strong confirmation. If they find nothing, the model needs revision.

**The distinction:** Hansen says "people believe there's more." The framework says "here's specifically where to look and why." That's the difference between a belief and a hypothesis.

## Why this matters

1. **The highest standard of falsifiability.** A prediction about physical reality that hasn't been checked yet — derived entirely from the geographic and institutional logic of the framework. Not explaining the past. Predicting what remains to be found.

2. **Appendix A showcase.** This is the strongest candidate for the future research appendix. It's specific enough that an archaeologist could act on it. It names locations, dates the expected material to a century, and explains the preservation mechanism. If confirmed, it's the most dramatic validation the framework could receive.

3. **The framework's final move in Hansen's material.** The last insight from the Silk Road seminar is a prediction about undiscovered physical evidence. The semester began with the framework explaining what we already knew. It ends with the framework telling us where to look for what we haven't found yet.
