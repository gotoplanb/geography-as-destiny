- The insight that Roman longevity is a geographic outlier explained by peer-competitor absence

## Appendix A — Rome Material (parked)

Two future research threads from this exchange:
1. **Closed maritime geometry and institutional longevity.** Do civilizations centered on closed/semi-closed maritime basins systematically outlast land empires facing open frontiers? Mediterranean (Rome), potentially Caribbean, South China Sea.
2. **Self-inflicted friction collapse as category.** Empires that defeat themselves by rationally optimizing away their own structural advantages. Constantinople. Potentially Abbasid relocation from Damascus to Baghdad. Mongol capital shifts. A pattern of locally rational decisions that are systemically catastrophic.
