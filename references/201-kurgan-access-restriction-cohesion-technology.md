## Anthony's Empirical Findings

Anthony reports specific patterns in the kurgan archaeological record:

- **Male-centered adult burials.** Most kurgan burials are adult males, suggesting selective rather than universal access to kurgan interment.
- **Female ratio approximately 1-in-5.** Women appear in the burial record, sometimes in war dress and leadership-role grave goods, suggesting that the access criteria were not strictly gendered but did select against universal access.
- **Rapid construction with intensive short-term use, followed by abandonment.** Kurgans were built quickly, used intensively for relatively brief periods (sometimes a few generations at most), then abandoned. The pattern is not "build slowly across centuries" but "build rapidly, use, abandon."

These are empirical patterns Anthony's archaeology documents. The framework reading interprets these patterns through structural mechanism.

## The Cohesion-Through-Restricted-Access Reading

The framework reads the demographic and use patterns as evidence of a specific social technology operating in the Yamnaya context:

**If anyone could claim any territory through kurgan burial, the federation would dissolve.** Every individual optimizes for their own claim, and the collective action capacity that makes the raiding-and-defense network function disappears. This is the tragedy of the commons operating on territorial claims rather than on grazing resources.

Restricting kurgan access to a warrior prestige class solves this problem elegantly. The pattern Anthony documents — selective adult burial with a documented female minority who reached prestige roles — fits exactly what the framework would predict if kurgan burial was the institutional reward for federation service:

- **The youth have a clear path to status.** Young men can demonstrate valor in koryos raiding (card 195's mechanism), accumulate cattle through bride-price exchange, and eventually earn the right to claim and mark territory through kurgan burial.
- **The restriction creates investment in collective enterprises.** Young men who could claim land freely would defect to individual optimization. Young men whose claims must be earned through federation service remain invested in the federation's raiding-and-defense network.
- **Female warriors in leadership roles fit the same pattern at the meritocratic level.** Persistent frontiers under sustained pressure (card 174) select for competence over gender convention. When survival requires maximum utilization of available talent, peacetime social restrictions become too costly. The Scythian Amazon tradition — probably mythologized but based on real female warrior burials — operates through the same mechanism for the same reason.

The kurgan is both reward and mechanism. The right to claim land is earned through service to the federation that makes claims defensible. The claim is only valuable because the federation enforces it. The federation is only cohesive because the claim is worth earning.

This is the koryos mechanism (card 195) operating at the longer-term institutional scale — not just raiding for bride price but the entire lifetime arc from youth warrior bands through eventual prestige burial with territorial claim function.

## The Frontier-Pressure-Wave Reading

The rapid construction + intensive use + abandonment pattern Anthony documents also fits the framework's expansion-phase reading:

**Kurgans get built rapidly at the leading edge of the expansion.** A population moves into new territory, buries its prestigious dead, establishes the territorial claim. The next generation pushes further. The old kurgan zone becomes internal territory where the claim is secure enough not to need continuous reinforcement through new burials.

Anthony's kurgan distribution maps therefore record the pressure wave's leading edge at any given moment. High kurgan-construction density at the current expansion frontier. Older zones with established claims and reduced new kurgan construction. The kurgan density mapping the position of the expansion front the same way meteorological pressure-front mapping records weather system positions.

This reading aligns with consensus archaeology — kurgan-density mapping is a standard tool for tracking Yamnaya expansion. The framework's contribution is making the underlying mechanism explicit: kurgans are the institutional marker that the federation has consolidated control of the territory, not just passed through it. Where construction density is high, the federation is actively claiming; where it has declined, the claim is established and the expansion has moved on.

## The Pattern as Wave Made Visible

The framework's reading suggests an analogical observation worth flagging: **the kurgan distribution is the Yamnaya pressure wave made visible in the archaeological record.** Like meteorological isobars marking pressure gradients, kurgan density gradients mark expansion-phase territory. Like marker buoys recording the leading edge of an advancing front, individual kurgan clusters mark the moments and places where the expansion consolidated new territory.

This is a structural analogy rather than a direct empirical claim. Anthony's evidence supports the underlying pattern (expansion-phase kurgans clustering at frontier zones); the "pressure wave made visible" framing is the framework's vocabulary for what the pattern represents. Useful framework formulation, not new empirical claim.
