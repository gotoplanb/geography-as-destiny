# Hämäläinen's Methodology — Independent Confirmation of the Wald Bomber Problem

**Source:** Hämäläinen, *The Comanche Empire*, methodology section
**Type:** fact
**Relevant to:** Chapter 6 (Parallel Conquests — footnote for methodology), ref 064 (Wald bomber)

## Content

Hämäläinen explicitly names the documentary bias problem in her own methodology:
- "Euro-imperial documentary spine" — the surviving record systematically overrepresents the European perspective
- "Lists actions but not the cultural motivations that drive the actions"
- Attempts "reconstruction of Comanche perspective" and acknowledges "missing voices of women and children"
- Uses ethnohistory, sidestepping and upstreaming as methodology to work around the bias
- Concerned about cultural determinism and preserving individual agency

## Why this matters

1. **Independent scholarly confirmation.** A leading historian of the Comanche, working from within the field, identifies the same documentary bias this book's framework diagnoses as structural. We don't have to assert the Wald bomber problem for the American frontier — Hämäläinen asserts it for us. Chapter 6 can cite her methodology statement as confirmation rather than making the claim independently.

2. **The zoom-level distinction.** Hämäläinen worries about taking away individual agency because she's writing about specific people making specific choices. Our framework operates at the structural level — the distribution of possible outcomes, not the choices within that distribution. Both valid. Neither cancels the other. "High-leverage draws from a loaded distribution" (ref 026) accommodates individual agency within structural constraint.

3. **For the footnote:** Cite Hämäläinen's methodology section as independent confirmation that the American frontier documentary record is sender-side encoded. The returning bombers are the Spanish/Mexican/American administrative records. The Comanche perspective is the engine hits she's trying to reconstruct.
