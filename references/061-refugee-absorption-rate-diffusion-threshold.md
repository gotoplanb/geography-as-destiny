# Refugee Absorption Rate as Diffusion Threshold — Slow Integrates, Fast Triggers Conflict

**Source:** Hansen, *The Silk Road* (Gandhara refugees at Miran), via Beta seminar 2026-04-19
**Type:** connection
**Relevant to:** Chapter 5 (Islamic Diffusion — Rogers applied), Chapter 1 (diffusion lens — addresses Charlie's #5)

## Content

Small groups of Gandhara refugees arriving incrementally at Tarim Basin oasis cities were absorbed without resistance. The thermodynamic pressure differential was low enough that the host system could integrate the input without defensive reorganization.

**The principle:** The rate of arrival determines the receiver-side response, not the content of who's arriving. Same people, same culture — but slow arrival integrates, fast arrival triggers conflict. This is Rogers applied to population dynamics:
- Early arrivals absorbed as curiosities or useful additions (innovators/early adopters in Rogers terms)
- If rate accelerates past a threshold, reception flips from acceptance to resistance (the system reorganizes defensively)

**Testable prediction:** Historical refugee and migration events should show this pattern. Slow diffusion integrates. Fast diffusion triggers conflict. The content is identical. The rate determines the outcome. Check against any migration event in the historical record.
