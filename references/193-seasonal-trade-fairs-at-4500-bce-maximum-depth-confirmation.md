## The Mechanism Restated

The seasonal trade fair emerges wherever three conditions are met simultaneously:

1. **A robust frontier exists** — two terrain types selecting for different economic adaptations producing bundles of opposed customs (card 174)
2. **The pressure differential is low enough** — neither side can simply extract from the other through pure raiding without sustained cost; both sides find exchange more profitable than coercion at the current power balance
3. **Seasonal cycles structure the exchange** — climate, agricultural calendar, or pastoral movement creates predictable windows when both populations can converge at neutral terrain

When all three conditions hold, the seasonal trade fair emerges automatically. No cultural invention required. No central authority directing the formation. Just the gradient logic producing convergent behavior across both populations at predictable times and locations.

The fair is the institutional form that the productive-exchange phase (card 192) takes wherever the three conditions are met. The hybrid material culture, specialized production settlements, partial miscibility — all of card 192's productive-exchange-phase signatures — accompany the fair as its material-culture footprint.

Across nearly seven thousand years, across multiple terrain types, across radically different cultural-political-technological contexts, the same institutional form recurs whenever the three structural conditions are met. The framework reads this as confirmation that the seasonal trade fair is a structural output of robust-frontier conditions, not a cultural invention transmitted by any specific tradition.

**Terrain creates the frontier. Frontier creates the exchange opportunity. Exchange opportunity produces the seasonal fair. Every time. Everywhere. Regardless of the specific cultures involved.**

This is the seasonal trade fair stated as universal framework principle. The chain is structural:

- **Geography** produces robust frontiers wherever two terrain types meet that select for different adaptations (card 174)
- **Pressure equilibrium** during the productive-exchange phase (card 192) makes exchange more profitable than raiding for both sides
