# Comanche Fail the Counterfactual Removal Test

**Source:** Dave's observation while reading Hämäläinen, 2026-04-23
**Type:** connection
**Relevant to:** Chapter 6 (Parallel Conquests — anti-great-man applied to the Comanche as a group), ref 026 (high-leverage draws)

## Content

Apply the counterfactual removal test (ref 003) at the group level: remove the Comanche entirely from history. Does the structural outcome change?

**The framework says no.** The resource gradient (horses + open grassland) still exists. Some other group — Utes, Apaches, or another Plains nation — fills the horse-mobility niche. Spain still declines because the structural exhaustion is independent of which specific group pressures the frontier. The railroad still wins because the technology differential is overwhelming regardless of which horse culture it faces.

The Comanche shaped the specific path — which territory was contested, which alliances formed, which battles happened where. They didn't shape the structural outcome: horse-mobile empire rises on the Plains, pressures colonial Spain, gets overrun by railroad-enabled expansion.

**The Peter/Tecumseh mirror applied to an entire people.** Peter compressed the conquest timeline. Tecumseh extended the resistance timeline. The Comanche shaped the specific character of Plains horse-empire politics. Remove any of them and the distribution produces the same structural outcome within a generation.

High-leverage draws from a loaded distribution. The distribution was loaded by the geography. The Comanche were the draw that happened. Another draw was possible. The distribution would have produced the same shape.

## Why this matters

Chapter 6, one paragraph: the counterfactual removal test applied to the Comanche as a group, not an individual. "Remove them. Does the outcome change? No. The gradient still exists. Someone fills it." This is the Chapter 1 framework (ref 026) confirmed on the flagship evidence chapter's primary case.
