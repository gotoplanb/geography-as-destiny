## The Framework's Predictions About PIE Spread

If the framework's logic from the Plains and Eurasian steppe cases applies to the Bronze Age, several predictions follow that Anthony should either support or contradict:

**1. PIE spread was a pressure wave, not a migration of a unified people.**

The conventional encoding — "the Indo-Europeans" as a unified people with a homeland who migrated outward carrying a shared language — is the sedentary category error from card 153 applied to prehistoric linguistic spread. Steppe pastoralist cultures don't form unified peoples in the nation-state sense. They form distributed networks of mobile bands sharing enough cultural and linguistic substrate to communicate and trade. The framework predicts Anthony will document this distributed-network structure rather than a unified-people structure.

**2. The lingua franca emerged because it solved coordination problems for trading partners.**

The framework's communication apparatus (Shannon-Rogers) predicts that languages don't spread because their speakers conquer territory; they spread because they solve receiver-side coordination problems in contact zones. The Comanche lingua franca on the southern Plains, Sogdian on the Silk Road, and PIE on the Bronze Age steppe should all show the same mechanism — adoption by receivers because the language solved a commercial or social problem the receivers had, not imposition by senders because senders dominated militarily.

**3. The "homeland" framing is a sedentary back-projection.**

PIE historiography has spent significant effort identifying "the Indo-European homeland" — Anatolia, the Pontic-Caspian steppe, various candidates. The framework predicts the question is partly malformed. There was no homeland in the nation-state sense. There was a corridor — likely the Pontic-Caspian steppe, but the corridor matters more than the point — through which the linguistic substrate developed and from which it spread by pressure-wave dynamics. The "where" question makes more sense as a "through which corridor" question than as a "from which capital" question.

**4. The Bronze Age corridors were operating before they were named.**

Dave's "Silk Road doing its thing forever" observation predicts that the corridor structure documented in the Han Dynasty period was operating long before the literate civilizations that named it existed. The Silk Road as a name dates from the 19th century. The corridor as a structure was operating commercially and culturally for millennia before any of the civilizations that used it were literate enough to document it. Anthony pushing the substrate back to ~4000 BCE should show the same corridor logic the framework documents in the Tang/Song period.

**5. Horse domestication was the conjunction-logic moment.**

The framework's conjunction logic (card 158) predicts that PIE spread should map onto a specific conjunction: Pontic-Caspian terrain + horse domestication technology + existing pastoralist cultural substrate + position outside the dominant Mesopotamian/Egyptian sedentary blocs. Remove any one variable and the spread doesn't happen at the documented scale. Anthony's title (*The Horse, the Wheel, and Language*) suggests this is exactly the conjunction he documents.

## The Khanate Analogy as Predictive Tool

The Khanate framing — a distributed network of mobile pastoralist bands sharing enough cultural and linguistic substrate to communicate and trade, without a unified political center — is the framework's preferred model for understanding pre-state pastoralist spreads. The Mongol confederation, the Comanche confederation, the Sogdian commercial network, and (predicted) the PIE substrate all share this structure.

If Anthony's evidence shows PIE spreading through similar distributed-network mechanisms, the Khanate analogy holds and the framework's reach extends back to 4000 BCE. If Anthony's evidence shows a unified-people migration with a single political center exporting language outward, the framework needs revision for the prehistoric case.

The framework's existing prediction is the former. The reading will test it.
