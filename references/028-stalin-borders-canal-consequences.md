# Stalin's Borders — Imperial Border-Drawing as Canal with Unintended Consequences

**Source:** Beta seminar discussion, 2026-04-14
**Type:** fact
**Relevant to:** Chapter 6 (Parallel Conquests — aftermath), ref 022 (delusion of control)

## Content

Samarkand and Bukhara were historically Persian-speaking (Tajik) cities. When Stalin drew Soviet republic borders, he deliberately mixed ethnicities to prevent any one republic from being too coherent — classic imperial strategy to maintain dependency.

Result: Tajik cities ended up inside Uzbekistan. That border decision is still generating tension between Uzbekistan and Tajikistan today.

The Tajiks are the ghost of pre-Turkic Central Asia — descendants of the original Persian-speaking sedentary population before the Turkic migrations.

**Framework connection:** Another canal with unintended consequences the river is still trying to correct. Colonial borders drawn without regard for underlying human geography are dams still cracking (ref 012, ref 022). The geographic/ethnic logic predates the border by millennia. The border is decades old. The river remembers.

## Why this matters

Concrete modern example of the variance explosion from model-system mismatch. Stalin's model: mix ethnicities → prevent nationalist coherence → maintain Moscow's control. Actual outcome: frozen ethnic tensions that erupted post-1991 and continue today. The omitted variable was that ethnic geography has more information than the border-drawer.
