## The Observation (Dave Stanton)

We know of Sogdian's emergence in Transoxiana not because anyone deliberately decreed it but because it solved a coordination need at a major contact zone. Sanskrit, even older, did similar work in another contact zone with phonological structure that may have been particularly accessible to speakers of other languages.

The Big Bang in modern physics is no longer treated as the absolute beginning of everything. It is the beginning of *our current observable universe.* Anything before it is hidden behind a singularity that destroys the information needed to reconstruct what preceded it. There may have been other universes. We can't know because the physics becomes non-functional beyond that horizon.

The same epistemic structure applies to pre-documentary lingua francas. Before a certain point in the historical record, the linguistic and archaeological evidence becomes too sparse to reconstruct what preceded it — not because nothing existed but because the preservation conditions destroy the information. If horses or comparable mobility technology existed in the Transoxiana corridor a couple thousand years before Sogdian emerged, the same coordination need almost certainly produced a lingua franca then too. We just can't see it directly.

## The Big Bang Epistemic Barrier Mapped to History

The two barriers share an identical structure:

| Cosmological | Historical |
|---|---|
| Singularity destroys information | Absence of writing destroys information |
| Physics non-functional beyond barrier | Reconstruction non-functional beyond barrier |
| Universe before is plausible but unobservable | Lingua franca before is plausible but unobservable |
| Indirect evidence (CMB, expansion patterns) | Indirect evidence (loanwords, archaeology, genetics) |
| Multiple universes plausible | Multiple lingua francas plausible |

The analogy is not loose. Both cases involve a horizon beyond which direct observation fails, indirect evidence still operates partially, and the underlying mechanism likely persists across the barrier even though the specific entities on each side are no longer recoverable.

## Each Lingua Franca as a Universe

Apply the analogy to historical lingua francas and the result is a recurring-universe model. Each lingua franca has:

- **A Big Bang** — emergence from contact pressure when mobile and sedentary populations meet at a material frontier and need a coordination medium
- **Expansion** — adoption by receiver populations because it solves their coordination problems, regardless of the speakers' military status
- **Heat death** — absorption into or replacement by the next pressure wave, often when the underlying contact zone shifts or the dominant trade pattern changes

The visible cases include Sogdian on the Silk Road (~5th–10th century CE), Aramaic in the Persian and Levantine corridor (~7th–4th century BCE), Akkadian in the Mesopotamian corridor (~24th–8th century BCE), Arabic across the post-Islamic diffusion zone, Latin across the Roman frontier, English across the contemporary global trade corridors. Each was the lingua franca of its zone. Each was succeeded by another when the zone's pressure structure shifted.

The pre-documentary cases are the framework's "previous universes":

- **Proto-Indo-European** (~4000–3000 BCE) — the lingua franca of the Pontic-Caspian steppe contact zone, operating before literacy reached the corridor in any sustained way. Anthony's reconstruction is the framework's clearest available pre-documentary universe.
- **Pre-PIE substrate languages** that PIE partially displaced and partially absorbed — detectable through loanwords in PIE that do not fit the reconstructed phonological patterns. The previous-previous universe, visible only through inherited fragments.
- **Whatever came before that** — invisible, but the framework predicts it existed wherever similar contact-zone pressures operated.

## What the Framework Predicts About the Pre-Documentary Period

If the lingua franca emergence mechanism is universal — receiver-side coordination problems being solved by adoption of a medium speakers find easier or more useful than fragmentation — then the framework predicts:

1. **Wherever sustained mobile-sedentary contact existed, a lingua franca probably emerged**, regardless of whether documentary evidence preserves it. The Pontic-Caspian steppe in 5000 BCE almost certainly had one. The Iranian plateau corridor in 4000 BCE almost certainly had one. The Indus Valley contact zones almost certainly had several across their history.

2. **The framework's reach into pre-documentary history is principled, not speculative.** It is asking what the contact-zone pressures would have required, and answering that the same pressures that produced Sogdian and Aramaic should have produced equivalents whenever the conditions were present.

3. **The visible cases are sampling artifacts of literacy, not anomalies.** Aramaic is not the first lingua franca; it is the first one for which the documentary preservation crossed a threshold. Sogdian is not particularly distinctive; it is documented because it operated in a period when major literate civilizations on either side of the corridor were keeping records.

## The Methodological Move

The Big Bang analogy gives the framework a vocabulary for pre-documentary claims that does not require either false certainty or epistemic surrender. The framework can say:

- We cannot directly observe the pre-PIE substrate lingua francas.
- We can confidently predict they existed, based on the universality of the mechanism.
- We can specify the conditions under which they would have operated and the contact zones that would have produced them.
- We can identify indirect evidence that confirms or modifies the prediction.

This is the same epistemic structure cosmology uses for the early universe. It is more than speculation. It is less than direct observation. It is principled inference from the mechanism applied to known boundary conditions.

**Each lingua franca is a universe. The steppe contact zone has been generating and consuming them for as long as mobile populations have been meeting sedentary ones at material frontiers. We see the ones the documentary record preserved. The framework predicts the ones it didn't.**
