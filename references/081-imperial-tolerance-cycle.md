# The Imperial Tolerance Cycle — Expansion Includes, Contraction Excludes

**Source:** Beta seminar discussion, 2026-04-22 (Hansen, Tang persecution of Sogdians)
**Type:** connection
**Relevant to:** Chapter 4 (Sogdians — why the operating system was destroyed), Chapter 1 (testable pattern across multiple empires)

## Content

**The cycle:**
1. **Expansion phase:** The empire needs administrators, merchants, translators, specialists. Can't afford ethnic exclusivity. Tolerance is economically rational. Identity boundaries deliberately porous to absorb talent from the periphery.
2. **Peak phase:** Institutional capacity becomes self-sufficient. Foreign specialists less necessary. Their presence shifts from useful to threatening. Identity boundaries begin to harden.
3. **Decline phase:** Resource gradient shrinking. Scapegoating minorities becomes politically useful for explaining decline without implicating the ruling class. Visibly foreign, commercially successful minorities are perfect targets.

**The Tang-Sogdian case:** Early Tang was extraordinarily cosmopolitan — Chang'an had Zoroastrian temples, Nestorian churches, Buddhist monasteries, Muslim mosques simultaneously. As Tang power peaked and began to recede (post-Talas 751, An Lushan Rebellion 755), the Sogdians became targets — visibly foreign, commercially successful, connected to the Islamic world displacing Tang influence westward. The persecution destroyed the merchant network that had sustained Tang prosperity. The empire consumed its own trade infrastructure while busy expressing ethnic anxiety.

**The pattern replicates independently:**
- **Rome:** Early inclusion (citizenship extended broadly, provincial elites absorbed) → late paranoia (persecution cycles, "barbarization" becoming threatening)
- **Mongol Empire:** Peak cosmopolitanism under Kublai Khan → successor khanates increasingly exclusive as they contracted
- **Ottoman Empire:** Early tolerance (welcomed Jewish refugees from Spain, Greek/Armenian merchant classes essential) → late exclusion (Armenian genocide, Greek population exchanges as empire contracted toward ethnic Turkish core)
- **British Empire:** Early pragmatic partnerships → late rigid racial hierarchy as economic justification weakened

**The bidirectional acceleration:** Decline produces scapegoating AND scapegoating accelerates decline. Destroying the diverse talent base and trading relationships that sustained competitiveness removes the very infrastructure the empire needs to reverse its contraction. The dam fails faster because the empire stopped maintaining it while persecuting the engineers.

**Thermodynamic framing:** Expanding systems can afford to keep identity boundaries porous because the incoming energy (resources, talent, trade) exceeds the cost of diversity. Contracting systems can't afford the energy cost of maintaining diversity and default to exclusion — which is locally cheaper but systemically destructive.

## Why this matters

1. **Chapter 4 gains explanatory depth.** The Sogdian dissolution (ref 076) wasn't just the Abbasid conquest removing their niche. The Tang were already turning against them before the full collapse. The persecution and the conquest were concurrent pressures from opposite directions.

2. **Testable pattern across independent empires.** Four cases (Tang, Rome, Mongol, Ottoman) showing the same cycle with no contact or coordination. Same thermodynamic logic, different geography, same sequence. The n is growing again.

3. **The reader will make the modern connection.** The editorial line (pre-9/11 = analyze, post-9/11 = reader's job) applies here. State the historical pattern. The reader applies it to the present. That's stronger than us doing it for them.
