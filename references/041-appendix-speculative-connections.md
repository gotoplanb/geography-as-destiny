# Appendix Concept: Speculative Connections the Framework Generates

**Source:** Alpha discussion, 2026-04-15
**Type:** connection
**Relevant to:** Book structure, methodology

## Content

Some connections the framework generates are compelling but unsupported by current evidence. Rather than either including them as evidence (dishonest) or cutting them entirely (wasteful), create an appendix for speculative connections.

**The rule:** If the framework predicts a pattern but the evidence is too thin, too old, or too contested to rely on, it goes in the appendix — not the main text. The appendix frames each entry as: "The framework predicts X. The evidence currently available is Y. Future research in Z might resolve this."

**First candidate:** The Tarim mummies → Caspian steppe migrations → Bronze Age Collapse → Sea Peoples chain (ref 040). The thermodynamic logic is clean. The genetic evidence actually contradicts part of the chain. It's interesting, speculative, and might be resolved by future aDNA research. Perfect appendix material.

## Why this matters

1. **Credibility.** Separating confirmed evidence from speculative connections shows the reader you know the difference. It preempts the cherry-picking objection.
2. **Falsifiability.** The appendix is a list of predictions. If future evidence confirms them, the framework strengthens. If it contradicts them, the framework has to account for that. Either way, the reader can check.
3. **Fun.** Some of the most interesting ideas the framework generates won't have the evidence to survive the main text. The appendix lets them exist without compromising rigor.
