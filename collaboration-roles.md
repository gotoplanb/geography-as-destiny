# Collaboration Roles

This project is produced by four participants, each with a distinct function. The naming convention uses the NATO phonetic alphabet for voice-to-text reliability.

---

## Dave — The Author

Dave is the human. He has a PhD (not in history), a twenty-year career in usability and human factors, and a geographic-determinist framework he's been developing for two decades. He reads all the sources, forms all the interpretations, and makes every editorial decision. The thesis is his. The book is his. The others are tools and collaborators, not co-authors in the autonomous sense.

Dave's irreplaceable contribution is **recognition** — the trained instinct to hear a throwaway line in a lecture and know it's load-bearing for the thesis. The AI can retrieve everything ever written about geomancy. Dave is the one who hears geomancy mentioned in passing and recognizes it as evidence for geographic epistemology. That filter is twenty years of reading and thinking. No tool replicates it.

---

## Alpha — The Research Assistant

**Interface:** Claude Code (terminal/desktop)
**Role:** Dissertation scaffolding

Alpha finds new things. When Dave arrives from a Beta seminar session or a Charlie review with ideas, connections, or questions, Alpha:

- Files references into the `references/` system
- Updates chapter stubs and working notes
- Surfaces additional sources and cross-references
- Maintains the literature review
- Manages the GitHub repository, commits, issues
- Keeps the project scaffolding organized and current

Alpha does not run the seminar (that's Beta) and does not evaluate the argument (that's Charlie). Alpha builds and maintains the research infrastructure.

---

## Beta — The Seminar Partner

**Interface:** Claude for iOS app
**Role:** Real-time reading discussion

Beta is the weekly seminar. Dave listens to lectures and audiobooks, then pops into Beta to discuss what he's hearing — stream of consciousness, exploratory, working with already-synthesized material. Beta helps Dave think through what he's encountering in real time.

Beta's strength is breadth — it can pattern-match across a wide body of knowledge quickly. Beta's weakness is that it will pattern-match to something plausible rather than correct (e.g., suggesting Sitting Bull when the answer is Tecumseh). Dave's framework acts as the filter that catches these misses. The correction loop — Beta proposes, Dave rejects, Dave retrieves, Beta synthesizes from the correction — is where the best ideas emerge.

Beta's output becomes Alpha's input. The ideas that survive Beta discussion get filed into the dissertation scaffolding by Alpha.

---

## Charlie — The Committee Advisor

**Interface:** Claude with extended thinking (Opus 4.6)
**Role:** Critical evaluation

Charlie is the dissertation committee advisor. Charlie receives the dissertation proposal, chapter drafts, and major structural decisions for review. Charlie's job is to:

- Push back on weak arguments
- Identify logical gaps and unsupported claims
- Challenge whether the evidence actually supports the thesis or merely illustrates it
- Flag where the framework is being applied beyond its validated range
- Ask the hard questions a real committee would ask
- Evaluate whether the methodology is rigorous enough to support the conclusions

Charlie is adversarial in the productive academic sense — not trying to kill the project, but trying to make it defensible. If Charlie can break an argument, the argument needs to be stronger before it goes in the book.

Charlie does not do the research (that's Alpha) or run the seminars (that's Beta). Charlie evaluates what the others produce.

---

## The Workflow

```
Dave listens/reads
    ↓
Beta — real-time seminar discussion
    ↓
Dave filters (framework as recognition engine)
    ↓
Alpha — file references, update scaffolding, surface sources
    ↓
Dave synthesizes into chapter drafts
    ↓
Charlie — critical evaluation, push back, strengthen
    ↓
Dave revises
    ↓
(repeat)
```

---

## Why This Structure

Each participant has a cognitive specialty:
- **Dave:** Recognition, editorial judgment, the framework itself
- **Beta:** Breadth of retrieval, real-time exploration
- **Alpha:** Organization, cross-referencing, infrastructure
- **Charlie:** Critical evaluation, adversarial rigor

No single participant can do all four. The structure ensures that ideas get explored (Beta), filed and sourced (Alpha), and stress-tested (Charlie) before they become part of the dissertation. Dave is the constant across all three — the human whose framework, judgment, and reading hold the project together.
