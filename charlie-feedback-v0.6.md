The compass cognitive quarantine and phase-zero extension of Rogers (085), the layered encoding / zero-hop argument (086), and the Atlantic crossing as oceanic parallel to the railroad (094) are among the strongest analytical moves in the project. They appear in endnotes and brief mentions but deserve more prominent treatment in the chapter text itself.

- Card 085 (phase zero) should be more than an endnote in Ch. 1 — it's a genuine contribution to diffusion theory
- Card 086 (layered encoding) should be more prominent in the methodology section — "The Pamir Knot cannot be moved to the British Museum" is the single clearest argument for the geographic approach
- Card 094 (Atlantic parallel) should appear in Ch. 1 Section V or Ch. 6 — it doubles the n on the parallel conquests pattern

**Action:** Promote these from endnote references to body-text arguments.

### #20 — The Dunhuang reinterpretation (090) needs explicit Appendix A placement

Card 090 (missionary training center vs. Hansen's cosmopolitanism) has grown across multiple updates into what is essentially a paper outline — with a competing interpretation, testable predictions, progressive silence as the decisive test, and archaeological implications. It's too developed to leave as a reference card and too bold to put in the main text without the primary source engagement it would require.

**Action:** Write it up as a formal Appendix A entry — hypothesis stated, test articulated, evidence needed, deferred to future research.

### #21 — Chapter 4 is now the longest chapter and may need structural editing

The integration pass added three new sections (Silence, Pax Mongolica, Corridor Goes Dark) to what was already the longest chapter. The chapter now spans from Richthofen to the Age of Sail — two thousand years of corridor history in ten sections. That's potentially too much for a single chapter.

**Consider:** Whether the post-Sogdian material (Sections VIII–X) should become a separate chapter, or whether the existing structure holds with tighter editing. The current pacing moves from archaeological detail (Sections I–IV) to macro-historical sweep (Sections VIII–X) in a way that may feel like two chapters stitched together.

**Not urgent.** The material is all strong. The question is architectural, not evidentiary.

### #22 — The predictive archaeology (097) should be the Appendix A showcase

Card 097 (predicting specific Tarim Basin nodes where sealed Buddhist caches should exist) is the most dramatic falsifiable prediction in the entire project. It names locations, dates the expected material, and explains the preservation mechanism. It should be the first entry in Appendix A — the demonstration that the framework generates actionable research predictions, not just retrospective explanations.

**Action:** Write the formal Appendix A entry for this prediction.

### #23 — Chapter 8 should reference the v0.6 milestone explicitly

The methodology chapter argues that the process trail is unprecedented. The v0.6 tag — six chapters drafted, 97 cards, 98+ endnotes, five committee reviews, 18 issues tracked — is itself evidence for that claim. A brief passage noting the project's own metrics would make the transparency argument concrete rather than abstract.
