## Seminar

**Valerie Hansen — *The Silk Road: A New History* (2013)**
Archaeologically grounded, focused on the Tarim Basin oasis cities (Dunhuang, Turfan, Khotan). Works from actual documents and artifacts rather than the usual macro narrative. More East Turkestan-focused than most Silk Road books. Fills the eastern half of the corridor that Tasar's seminar left underexplored.

---

## Reading List

### 1. Peter Frankopan — *The Silk Roads: A New History of the World* (2015)
The corridor-as-world-spine argument. Frankopan recenters world history on Central Asia and the Middle East rather than Europe. Directly relevant to Chapter 4 (Silk Road as Circulatory System). Where Hansen gives archaeological detail, Frankopan gives the macro sweep — the two complement each other as seminar + deep read.

**Focus while reading:** The Silk Road's geographic structure — two funnels, two fans, the Pamir Knot. How terrain shaped what could move and how fast. Where does Frankopan's narrative confirm or challenge our corridor model?

### 2. S. Frederick Starr — *Lost Enlightenment* (2013)
Carried over from semester 1 reading list. The Central Asian golden age — al-Khwarizmi, Ibn Sina, Ulugh Beg. Addresses Charlie's issue #3 (Timur as counterexample) directly. The question: was the intellectual output dynasty-dependent or terrain-dependent?

**Focus while reading:** The geography of Transoxiana — why this terrain produced this concentration. The decline after the Mongols — geographically conditioned or contingent? Evidence for or against the Timurid Paradox chapter.

### 3. Everett Rogers — *Diffusion of Innovations* (5th ed., 2003)
Addresses Charlie's issue #5 (diffusion lens underdeveloped) directly. The primary source for the third lens. Read with the Battle of Talas paper diffusion and Islamic trade-route spread as worked examples to test against.

**Focus while reading:** The S-curve, adopter categories, and adoption determinants — can they be applied to pre-modern historical cases with intellectual honesty? Where does the framework fit and where does it strain? Build at least one formal Rogers analysis during this semester.
